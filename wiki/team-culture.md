# Building Teams and Culture in Startups

Why people matter more than anything else in a startup.

## Hiring Philosophy

### Hire Slowly, Fire Fast

**Hire slowly**:
- Getting the first 10 people right is critical
- One bad hire at early stage is devastating
- Invest time in finding right person
- Test people before committing (projects, trials, short contracts)

**Fire fast**:
- If someone isn't working out, make decision quickly
- Bad hires damage team morale and culture
- Dragging out decision hurts the person and company
- Give clear feedback and help them succeed first

### What to Look For in Early Hires

1. **Competence** — Can they do the job? (Most important early)
2. **Motivation** — Do they want to be here and care about mission?
3. **Cultural fit** — Will they thrive in startup environment?
4. **Learning ability** — Can they grow into bigger roles?
5. **Resourcefulness** — Can they get things done with limited resources?

**Not as important early**:
- Pedigree or resume (can be misleading)
- Years of experience (hunger matters more)
- Credentials (ability matters more)

### Finding Great People

**Sources**:
- Your personal network (best source)
- Previous colleagues
- Online communities and forums
- Angel investors and advisors
- Other founders
- University networks
- Referral bonuses

**Recruiting funnel**:
1. Source candidates (multiple channels)
2. Phone screen (basic fit, interest)
3. Take-home or project task (show skills)
4. Interview (talk to team, assess culture fit)
5. Reference checks (verify claims)
6. Offer negotiation
7. Onboarding and success

## Early Team Structure

### The Founding Team (Before Employees)

**Co-founder dynamics**:
- Complementary skills (technical + business, ideally)
- Shared values and vision
- Ability to communicate and resolve disagreements
- Committed for long term
- Equal or clear ownership structure

**Co-founder mistakes**:
- 50/50 splits with unclear roles (leads to gridlock)
- Hiding disagreements (explodes later)
- Not discussing equity upfront
- Wrong partner (personality clash)

**Equity vesting**:
- Standard: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- If someone leaves in year 1, they get nothing (at cliff)
- Then 3 years of monthly vesting
- Protects company if co-founder leaves early

### First Employees (Employees 1-5)

Hire people who:
- Are self-directed (you can't babysit)
- Wear multiple hats (fill gaps, not just one function)
- Are motivated by impact (might not get paid market rate)
- Believe in mission (not just the job)

**Key early roles**:
- **Technical** (engineering/product): Build the product
- **Sales/Customer success**: Talk to customers, iterate
- **Operations/business**: Finance, legal, process, fundraising
- **Marketing**: Product messaging, content, positioning

You don't need to hire all at once. Many startups start with co-founders only, add first employee at 6-12 months.

### Growth Stage (Employees 6-50)

Start building functional departments:
- Engineering team with lead/senior engineers
- Sales and customer success team
- Marketing/communications
- Finance and operations
- People/HR function

Establish clear roles, responsibilities, and reporting structure.

## Compensation and Equity

### Salary Considerations

**Early stage typically pays below market** because:
- Less cash (bootstrapping or early seed)
- Equity is the upside
- Impact/learning is valuable

**Market ranges**:
- For bootstrapped: 30-60% of market rate
- For seed-funded: 50-80% of market rate
- For Series A+: 80-100% of market rate

**How to position below-market compensation**:
- Generous equity package (if venture-backed)
- Clear path to market salary as company grows
- Flexibility and impact
- Learning and growth opportunity
- Amazing team and culture

### Equity (Stock Options)

**Why equity matters**:
- Aligns incentives (employees want company to succeed)
- Deferred comp (no cash required)
- Skin in the game (people are committed)

**How it works**:
- You set up option pool (typically 10-20% reserved for employees)
- Grant options to employees
- Options vest over 4 years with 1-year cliff
- Employees buy shares at exercise price (typically FMV at grant)
- Shares worth money if company exits

**Option value depends on**:
- Company success (startup could be worthless)
- Exit timing and amount
- How much company raises (dilutes options)
- Your negotiated strike price

**Equity negotiation**:
- Earlier stage = higher equity percentage but more risk
- More senior/critical role = higher equity
- Supply and demand (hard to hire = offer more equity)
- Standard ranges:
  - Head of function: 0.5-2%
  - Senior IC: 0.25-0.75%
  - Mid-level: 0.1-0.25%
  - Junior: 0.05-0.1%

## Culture and Values

### Why Culture Matters

**Early**: Culture is who you are
- Founders set the culture by how they act
- Small team (everyone knows everyone)
- Decisions reflect company values

**Growth**: Culture is how you scale
- Processes codify values
- New people learn culture through onboarding
- Culture prevents drift as you grow

### Building Culture

1. **Define values** (3-5 core values)
   - Should reflect your beliefs and how you want to operate
   - Examples: customer obsession, bias for action, radical transparency

2. **Model values** as founders
   - You are the culture
   - Live the values daily
   - Call out value-aligned and value-misaligned behavior

3. **Hire for culture fit**
   - People should share core values
   - "Brilliant jerks" don't work long-term
   - Interview for values during hiring

4. **Rituals and traditions**
   - All-hands meetings where people connect
   - Team building (but keep it genuine, not forced)
   - Celebrations of wins
   - Retrospectives on failures

5. **Communication**
   - Be transparent about company progress, challenges
   - Share information widely
   - Don't keep people guessing
   - Admit when you don't know something

### Common Startup Culture Mistakes

- **Founder ego**: Founder needs to be right, shuts down disagreement
- **No feedback culture**: People can't tell you when you're wrong
- **Overwork culture**: Glorifying 80-hour weeks (unsustainable)
- **No diversity**: Everyone looks and thinks the same
- **Dishonesty**: Leadership isn't truthful about situation
- **No psychology safety**: People afraid to raise concerns or try things

## Remote Work

### Remote-First Startups

**Pros**:
- Larger talent pool (not geographically limited)
- More flexible, better work-life balance
- Often lower overhead (no office)
- Easier to scale

**Cons**:
- Harder to build culture
- Harder to onboard new people
- Requires more intentional communication
- Time zones can be challenging

### Making Remote Work

- **Async-first**: Assume not everyone is online same time
- **Written decisions**: Document decisions and reasoning
- **Over-communicate**: When in doubt, communicate more
- **Regular connection**: Intentional time together (quarterly offsites)
- **Timezone boundaries**: Respect people's time zones

## Advisor Relationships

### Why Advisors Matter

Good advisors:
- Provide strategic advice
- Introduce you to investors, customers, talent
- Lend credibility to company
- Help with specific expertise gaps

### Getting Advisors

- Ask successful people you know/respect
- Start with specific ask (help with fundraising, product, etc.)
- Be clear about what you need
- Offer small equity (0.05-0.5%)
- Respect their time (monthly call, not constant asks)

### Advisor Structure

- **Advisor agreement**: Equity, vesting, termination
- **Frequency**: Usually monthly call or less
- **Expectations**: Clear on what you're asking
- **Term**: Typically 2-3 years
