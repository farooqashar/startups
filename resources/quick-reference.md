# Startup Quick Reference Guide

Quick lookup tables and checklists for common startup tasks.

## Startup Metrics at a Glance

### Early Stage Targets (Pre-PMF)
| Metric | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Day 7 Retention | 40%+ | 50%+ |
| Day 30 Retention | 20%+ | 30%+ |
| Monthly Active Users (growth) | 5% MoM | 10%+ MoM |
| NPS (if product) | 20+ | 40+ |
| Customer interviews/month | 10+ | 20+ |

### Growth Stage Targets (Post-PMF)
| Metric | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Month-over-month growth | 10% | 20%+ |
| Day 30 Retention | 30%+ | 40%+ |
| CAC Payback Period | <12 months | <6 months |
| LTV/CAC Ratio | 3x | 5x+ |
| Churn Rate (monthly) | <5% | <2% |
| Net Dollar Retention | 100% | 110%+ |

### SaaS Specific
| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Gross Margin | 70%+ |
| Magic Number (ARR increase / Sales spend) | >0.75 |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | 6-12 months to payback |
| Annual Contract Value (ACV) | Product-dependent |
| Expansion Revenue | 10-20% of revenue |

## Fundraising Timeline

### If Seeking Institutional Capital (VC)
| Timeline | Activities |
|----------|-----------|
| Months 1-2 | Prepare materials: pitch deck, financials, 1-pager |
| Months 2-3 | Build investor list: identify 100+ relevant VCs |
| Months 2-5 | Pitch process: meetings, demos, calls |
| Months 3-6 | Due diligence: investors investigate |
| Months 4-6 | Term sheet negotiation and closing |

**Total time**: 3-6 months typically

### If Bootstrapping or Angel Funding
| Timeline | Activities |
|----------|-----------|
| Continuous | Talk to potential customers (no timeline) |
| Ongoing | Build product and get to MVP |
| Once MVP ready | Launch and measure product-market fit |
| At 3-6 months | Evaluate if fundraising needed |
| If fundraising | Get warm introductions to angels |

## Hiring Guide

### When to Hire Each Role

| Role | When to Hire |
|------|-------------|
| **VP Engineering** | Series A, if you're not technical founder |
| **First Sales Person** | When you have product-market fit signal |
| **Marketing** | Early, but often founder's job initially |
| **Finance/Accounting** | Series A or when complexity requires |
| **Head of Product** | Series B or when growth requires full PM |
| **VP Operations** | Series B/C when processes get complex |

### Typical Early Hires (First 10 people)
1. **CTO or Lead Engineer** (if founder is non-technical)
2. **Product/Design Lead** (if founder is non-product)
3. **Sales/Business Development**
4. **Customer Success/Support**
5. **Operations/Business Manager**
6. More engineers/specialists depending on market

### Compensation Ranges (Relative to Market Rate)

| Stage | Base Salary | Equity |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Bootstrapped** | 50-70% | 0.5-2% |
| **Seed Stage** | 70-85% | 0.25-1% |
| **Series A** | 85-100% | 0.1-0.5% |
| **Series B+** | 100%+ | 0.05-0.2% |

Equity percentages depend on seniority. Head of function gets more than IC.

## Pitch Deck Template

### Slide Order (12-15 slides)
1. **Title Slide** — Company name, tagline, your names
2. **Problem** — What's broken in the world?
3. **Solution** — How do you fix it?
4. **Market Opportunity** — How many people have this problem?
5. **Product** — What did you build?
6. **Traction** — Proof it works (users, revenue, partnerships)
7. **Business Model** — How do you make money?
8. **Competition** — Who else is doing this?
9. **Team** — Who are you? Why will you succeed?
10. **Financial Projections** — Revenue/expense forecasts
11. **Why Now** — Why is this the right time?
12. **The Ask** — How much are you raising? What for?
13. **(Optional) Use of Funds** — How will you use the money?

### Tips for Each Slide
- **Problem**: Make it concrete (story or statistics)
- **Solution**: Show, don't just describe
- **Market**: Start big, then focus on serviceable market
- **Product**: Live demo if possible, otherwise great screenshots
- **Traction**: Real numbers (revenue > users, revenue > press mentions)
- **Competition**: Show your differentiation, not why competitors don't exist
- **Team**: Focus on relevant experience, not just credentials

## Valuation Quick Reference

### Pre-Revenue/Pre-Traction
- 10-20x estimated annual revenue (if you have revenue projection)
- Comparables to similar stage companies
- Often ranges $500K - $5M

### Early Revenue (First Year)
- 5-10x annual revenue
- Example: $100K revenue × 7 = $700K valuation

### Growth Stage (Proven Model)
- 10-20x annual recurring revenue (for SaaS)
- Example: $100K MRR ($1.2M ARR) × 15 = $18M valuation

### Enterprise/B2B SaaS
- ARR multiple typical:
  - Series A: 8-12x ARR
  - Series B: 10-15x ARR
  - Series C: 12-20x ARR

### Important Notes
- Valuation is largely negotiation
- More important than valuation: terms, investor quality
- Ownership post-round is key: your % = investment / post-money valuation

## Common Equity Dilution Breakdown

Typical founder ownership after each round:

| Round | Amount | Dilution | Founder Ownership |
|-------|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Pre-raise** | N/A | 0% | 100% |
| **Seed** | $250K-1M | 10-20% | 80-90% |
| **Series A** | $1-5M | 20-30% | 56-72% |
| **Series B** | $5-15M | 25% | 42-54% |
| **Series C** | $15-50M | 20-30% | 29-43% |

Note: Each round dilutes all shareholders proportionally. These are ranges; actual dilution varies.

## Decision Making Frameworks

### Should We Raise Capital?

| Factor | Raise Capital | Bootstrap |
|--------|---|---|
| **Market size** | Large (>$1B) | Small/medium |
| **Time to PMF** | Uncertain | Clear path |
| **Competitive intensity** | High | Low |
| **Capital requirements** | High (product, sales) | Low |
| **Team ambitions** | Maximize growth | Sustainability |
| **Exit goals** | Acquisition/IPO | Profitability |

### When to Pivot

**Pivot if**:
- Core metric (retention) not improving despite iteration
- Customer interviews show problem isn't as big
- Better opportunity emerged from customer conversations
- Business model is unsustainable

**Don't pivot if**:
- You haven't tested enough (need 20+ customer interviews)
- You haven't given idea enough time (6+ months)
- You're just bored or want to chase shiny object
- Market/product fits but needs more time to grow

## Quick Checklists

### Pre-Launch Checklist
- [ ] Validated problem with 20+ potential customers
- [ ] Built MVP (can use no-code tools)
- [ ] Clear onboarding and how product works
- [ ] Way to collect feedback (email, form)
- [ ] Analytics setup (Google Analytics at minimum)
- [ ] Way to reach out if needed issues

### Series A Readiness Checklist
- [ ] $10K+ ARR minimum (proof of concept)
- [ ] 30%+ monthly growth for 3+ months
- [ ] >50% customer retention at 6 months
- [ ] Clear unit economics (CAC, LTV)
- [ ] Team of at least 3-5 people
- [ ] Professional financial projections
- [ ] Customer references who love your product

### Scaling Checklist (When to Hire)
- [ ] PMF validated (high retention, growth)
- [ ] Revenue or funding to cover salaries (12+ months)
- [ ] Specific problem to hire for (not just growth)
- [ ] Process for recruiting and onboarding
- [ ] CEO or founder can commit to hiring/managing
- [ ] Role, responsibilities, and expectations clear

## Quick Definitions

**MRR**: Monthly recurring revenue (subscription pricing × active customers)

**ARR**: Annual recurring revenue (MRR × 12)

**CAC**: Customer acquisition cost (total sales/marketing spend ÷ new customers)

**LTV**: Lifetime value (average revenue per customer × average customer lifetime)

**Churn**: % of customers who cancel per month

**Runway**: Months until cash runs out (current cash ÷ monthly burn rate)

**Burn rate**: Monthly cash spending

**PMF**: Product-market fit (when customers want and use your product)

**MVP**: Minimum viable product (smallest product to test hypothesis)

**Pivot**: Change direction (product, market, business model)

**Series A, B, C**: Funding rounds (A is first large VC round, B and C are subsequent rounds)

**Valuation**: What company is worth (used to determine equity percentage)

**Dilution**: Your ownership % decreases as company raises rounds

**Option pool**: Equity reserved for future employee stock options (typically 10-20%)

**Vesting**: Equity earned over time (typically 4 years, 1-year cliff)
